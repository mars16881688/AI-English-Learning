# Lesson 01: What is Artificial Intelligence?

## Reading （阅读难度：★★☆☆☆）

Artificial Intelligence, or AI, is a branch of computer science that focuses on creating machines that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. These tasks include understanding language, recognizing images, making decisions, and solving problems.

The idea of AI has been around for decades. In 1956, a group of scientists held a conference at Dartmouth College, where they first used the term "Artificial Intelligence." Since then, AI has gone through periods of excitement and disappointment, often called "AI summers" and "AI winters."

Today, AI is everywhere in our daily lives. When you use a voice assistant like Siri, it uses AI to understand your speech. When Netflix recommends a movie, it uses AI to predict what you might enjoy. When your email filters out spam, that is also AI at work.

There are two main types of AI. **Narrow AI** (also called Weak AI) is designed to perform a specific task. All the AI we use today is narrow AI. **General AI** (also called Strong AI) would be a machine that can think and learn like a human across any domain. General AI does not exist yet, and researchers disagree about when — or if — it will be achieved.

Modern AI is largely powered by **machine learning**, a technique where computers learn from data instead of being explicitly programmed. The most powerful form of machine learning today is **deep learning**, which uses artificial neural networks inspired by the human brain.

AI is transforming industries from healthcare to finance, from education to entertainment. Understanding AI is not just useful for engineers — it is becoming essential knowledge for everyone.
